First Solvay Conference in 1911. Max Planck is located, in the back row, second from the left.

A Genius

biografy

He was born on May 25, 1858 in Kiel, from the marriage of Julius Wilhem with his second wife Emma Patzig (1821-1914). He had four siblings (Hermann, Hildegard, Adalbert and Otto) and two half-siblings (Hugo and Emma), children of his father by his first wife.

Planck enrolled for the academic year 1874/75 at the Physics Faculty of the University of Munich. There, under the tutelage of Professor Jolly, Planck conducted his own experiments.

The 1877/78 academic year was carried out in Berlin, at the Friedrich-Wilhelm University , where he was taught by the famous physicists Hermann von Helmholtz and Gustav Kirchhoff , whom he had already met in Munich.

at the age of twenty-one. He returned to Munich in 1880 to teach at the university. In 1889, he returned to Berlin, where from 1892 he was the director of the chair for theoretical physics.

Professor at the University of Berlin

In Berlin, Planck entered the local Physical Society.
Planck was a teacher in Berlin for 37 years and was appreciated by his students as a teacher, according to Lise Meitner he was "dry, a bit impersonal", "using no notes, no mistakes, never wavering, the best teacher I have ever heard". He did not originate a true "school" because there were few graduate students.

Nobel Prize

From 1905 to 1909, Planck was the head of the Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft (German Physical Society). In 1913, he became head of the Berlin university. In 1918 he received the Nobel Prize in Physics for the creation of quantum mechanics.

Death

During World War II , Planck tried to convince Adolf Hitler to allow the work of Jewish scientists.

The Nazi regime and the war hit the Planck family hard. He lost his son Erwin Planck , assassinated by the Nazis on January 3, 1945, despite requests for pardon and mercy that Max Planck made to Hitler, trying to save the life of his son. This great loss was added to another, which he had just suffered: his house in Berlin-Grünewald (and with it his entire library, with thousands of volumes and irrecoverable manuscripts), was burned down and completely destroyed by an aerial bombardment in February. from 1944.
Following Max Planck's death on October 4, 1947 in Göttingen , the KWG changed its name to the Max-Planck Society

Legacy

Planck's discoveries, which were subsequently verified by other scientists, represented the birth of an entirely new field of physics, known as quantum mechanics , and provided the foundation for research in fields such as atomic energy

He recognized in 1905 the importance of the ideas on the quantification of electromagnetic radiation expounded by Albert Einstein , with whom he collaborated throughout his career.
